Our Family

The Lord has blessed Molly and I with a wonderful family. We have three beautiful little girls each with her own unique personality and talents. Both Molly and I come from relatively large families, and we always knew that we would want a large family for our own. From the moment our family started, we made the promise to one another that Christ would be the focal point of our marriage and upbringing of our children. Now, we've been plessed with the opportunity to bring another little girl into our family, to show her the love that God has shown us, and to raise her in the teachings of Christ.

Why Adoption?

We believe that adoption is a beautiful representation of was Christ has done for us in our lives. Through no goodness of our own, God chose us to become His sons and daughters and heirs to His blessings. With this picture in mind, we looked at the need of international adoption as a way for us to display the work of Christ in us and find our role within the Great Commission. By bringing this child into our family, we will have the opportunity to raise her in a Christian home where she can hear and see the goodness of God. We're so thankful that God has blessed us enough to be able to pursue this goal, and with the help of family and friends, we're sure we'll be able to achieve it.

Why Taiwan?

During the initial process, we tried to determine where the greatest need for adoption would be. We looked at many different factors, but the major one for Molly and I was Gospel saturation. How many of the people group was Christian (and in turn, how likely would a child raised there become a Christian)?

After looking at many different countries, a few being removed from our list because of restrictions we were unable to meet (age, size of family, etc.), we were led to adopt from Taiwan. According to a 2005 census, only 3.9% of Taiwan is professing Christian. Buddhism is the largest religion at 35.1% followed by Taoism at 33%. We also found during our research that many missionaries were being removed from Taiwan for financial reasons. This made it all the more apparent to us that there was a high probability that a child growing up in Taiwan would not come to know Jesus Christ as Savior.
